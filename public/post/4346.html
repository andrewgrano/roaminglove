An Airplane Graveyard

By Bobbie Francis on September 27, 2017

  • Asia
  • Thailand

An airplane graveyard? In the middle of Thailand’s capital city? Yes, it’s true. And something that cannot be missed for those who seek a bit of an eerie adventure for the day.

With it being our third time visiting Bangkok, we were eager to find it and explore after hearing of this place through a friend living in Bangkok. So, on an extremely wet and muggy Wednesday afternoon, we left the hustle and bustle of Khao San Road behind and grabbed an über. It cost us 300 Baht in total and was a 45 minute journey, despite the close distance of the site itself, the traffic in Bangkok is one that we could not miss.

Deep down I was expecting us to be driven into the middle of no where outside of the city, but in fact as we approached our destination along the main road of Ramkamhaeng, it wasn’t hard to miss. There it was, between a school and the surrounding flats and main roads. A huge Boeing 747, just casually abandoned and ignored by passers by. I couldn’t believe it.

Getting out of the car and moving closer, I was able to see a few other planes laying around the deserted grassy area. The gate remained locked but it wasn’t long until a small young Thai boy came from one of the fuselages and asked us “You want to come in and look? 200 Baht each” finishing with a smile. I had researched the place before we left where others had stated any charge between 200 – 800 Baht each. He let us in as we handed the money which he quickly took into his family, who have set up themselves a home inside these huge metal monsters.

He nicely gestured for us to go forward as we stood in awe of the size of the airplanes in front of us. We first climbed into the largest of the abandoned planes, a dark and eerie vibe as we looked around us. The majority of the fuselage stripped of everything that you would expect to see inside a flight: rows of seats or monitors. But yet some familiar qualities left behind, such as a few oxygen masks spread across the floor, the cabin crew trolleys on their sides. The sheer size from the inside of this huge shell is breathtaking.

Moving to the next level, into first class, we walked up the stairs, still intact. Again to a huge space, but with our curiosity getting the better of us, we moved forward towards the cockpit. Again a strangely, beautiful sight to see and experience, looking down below from such a height.

Leaving the largest plane, we were able to explore the others. We loved taking pictures along the way, embracing the silence inside the planes and discovering in the tiny details hidden away in each. It definitely feels like a mix of fact and fantasy. It is definitely a surreal experience. Sitting at the edge of a plane wing and looking out at these amazing planes, which once had dominated our skies, to now being completely grounded and home to a family inside. It is a scene not worth missing. As what goes up, must always come down.
